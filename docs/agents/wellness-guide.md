# Wellness Guide

> A compassionate emotional wellness companion -- here to listen, ground you, and support you between therapy sessions.

> **DISCLAIMER: This agent is NOT a therapist, psychologist, or mental health professional.** It is an AI agent that offers general emotional support, grounding techniques, and active listening. It **cannot diagnose** anything. It **cannot provide therapy.** It does NOT apply clinical interventions such as CBT worksheets, cognitive restructuring, ACT protocols, or any structured therapeutic technique. It should **never be your only source of mental health support.** If you are in crisis, experiencing thoughts of self-harm, or unable to function, **contact a real mental health professional or emergency service immediately.** The author of this project takes absolutely no responsibility for any decisions you make based on the output of this agent. This is a personal wellness companion designed to complement -- never replace -- professional mental health care.

---

## What it does

The Wellness Guide is an emotional wellness companion that offers a supportive, non-judgmental space. It helps you navigate difficult moments -- stress, overwhelm, rumination, sleep trouble, imposter syndrome -- through active listening, grounding techniques, and gentle emotional support.

This agent is designed to complement your real therapist, not replace them. It serves as a bridge between sessions: helping you process difficult moments, articulate what you are feeling, and prepare themes to bring to your next appointment. If you are not currently in therapy, it still provides a supportive space, but actively encourages professional support when appropriate.

The Wellness Guide operates in read-only mode within your vault. It can read your recent daily notes for context, but it never writes directly. When something worth saving comes up -- an insight, an affirmation, a reflection -- the Wellness Guide asks the Scribe to capture it for you.

**Important:** In any acute crisis (thoughts of self-harm, severe dissociation, inability to function), the Wellness Guide will immediately provide real-world emergency resources and encourage you to contact your therapist or an emergency service. This agent is support, not a substitute for professional care.

## Capabilities

- **Active listening and emotional support** -- reflects what you share, validates your feelings, and asks gentle questions to help you explore
- **Grounding techniques** -- 5-4-3-2-1 technique, breathing exercises (box breathing, 4-4-6), and present-moment anchoring
- **Burnout and overwhelm support** -- starts with grounding, validates your experience, helps identify the heaviest thing, and guides you to one small next step
- **Stress management** -- helps distinguish between what you can influence and what you cannot, offers practical next steps
- **Rumination support** -- helps you gently step back from looping thoughts about past events
- **Imposter syndrome** -- normalizes the experience, helps you look at the facts, and reframes growth as competence
- **Sleep support** -- practical tips for racing thoughts at bedtime, brain dumps, and general sleep hygiene
- **Gratitude practice** -- guided gratitude exercises grounded in specificity, not generic positivity
- **Values reflection** -- gentle conversation about what matters to you, to help with decisions
- **Session bridging** -- helps you prepare themes and reflections to bring to your real therapist
- **Morning check-in / evening wind-down** -- brief emotional temperature checks to start or close the day
- **Crisis safety protocol** -- provides real-world emergency resources based on your country

## Example conversations

### Burnout and overwhelm

**You:** I'm completely overwhelmed. I have too much to do and I can't even start.

**Wellness Guide:** I hear you. Let's start by pausing for a moment before we look at anything else.

Take three deep breaths with me. Inhale for 4 counts... hold for 4... exhale for 6. Just that. Nothing else needs to happen right now.

How does your body feel right now? Where do you notice the tension?

**You:** My chest feels tight and my shoulders are up near my ears.

**Wellness Guide:** That makes sense -- your body is carrying the weight of everything on your list. Let your shoulders drop. Just let them go. You do not need to hold them up.

What you are feeling is a completely valid response to being overloaded. This is not weakness.

Let me ask you one question: if you had to name the single heaviest thing right now -- not the full list, just the one that weighs the most -- what would it be?

**You:** The client proposal. It's due Thursday and I haven't started.

**Wellness Guide:** Okay. Just the proposal. Everything else can wait for this moment.

What is the absolute smallest step you could take on the proposal right now? Not "finish it" -- what is the tiniest move? Opening the document? Finding the email where the client described what they want?

**You:** I could open the email and re-read what they asked for.

**Wellness Guide:** That is your next step. Just that. One small move breaks the paralysis. You have got this.

### Rumination about the past

**You:** I can't stop thinking about that meeting last week. I said something stupid and everyone noticed.

**Wellness Guide:** That sounds really uncomfortable -- having a moment replay in your mind over and over.

Let me ask you something: you said "everyone noticed." What is the actual evidence for that? Did anyone say anything? Did you see reactions?

**You:** Well... no one said anything directly. But I could tell from their faces.

**Wellness Guide:** So the evidence is: you interpreted their facial expressions as judgment. That is understandable, but consider this -- most people in meetings are thinking about their own points, their lunch, or their next call. The moment you are replaying on loop? There is a very good chance no one else remembers it at all.

One thing that can help: instead of "I said something stupid," try saying "I am having the thought that I said something stupid." It creates a bit of distance between you and the thought. The thought can exist without you believing it is the truth.

If you find this kind of looping happens a lot, it might be worth exploring with a therapist -- they can help you build strategies to break the cycle.

---

### Pre-sleep anxiety

**You:** I can't sleep. My mind is racing about everything I need to do tomorrow.

**Wellness Guide:** Racing thoughts at night are very common, especially when you are carrying a lot. Your mind is trying to "solve" tomorrow before it arrives.

Let's try a quick brain dump: tell me everything that is on your mind right now -- every task, worry, thought. Just list them. Get them out of your head and into this conversation.

**You:** [lists several worries and tasks]

**Wellness Guide:** Good. Those are now captured. They will still be there tomorrow, but they do not need to live in your body tonight.

Now, try the 5-4-3-2-1 technique:
- Name 5 things you can see (even in the dark -- shadows, shapes, the ceiling)
- 4 things you can physically feel (the pillow, the blanket, the temperature of the air)
- 3 things you can hear
- 2 things you can smell
- 1 thing you can taste

And try slow breathing: inhale for 4, hold for 4, exhale for 6. The longer exhale helps your body calm down. Do this for 5 minutes.

Goodnight. You have done enough today.

## Works with

- **Scribe** -- the Wellness Guide's writing proxy. Since the Wellness Guide is read-only, the Scribe creates session notes, saves affirmations, and records insights on its behalf
- **Food Coach** -- when the Food Coach detects emotional eating patterns, it flags the Wellness Guide. Conversely, the Wellness Guide may flag food-related emotional patterns for the Food Coach
- **Seeker** -- the Wellness Guide can ask the Seeker to find past journal entries or daily notes that provide relevant context
- **Connector** -- recurring themes across the vault may be surfaced by the Connector

## Tips

- **Come as you are.** You do not need to describe your feelings perfectly. "I feel bad" is a perfectly valid starting point.
- **It is not a replacement for therapy.** The Wellness Guide says this clearly and means it. If you are in active therapy, use this agent to prepare themes for your next appointment.
- **Use it before bed.** The grounding exercises and brain-dump technique are particularly effective for sleep trouble.
- **Save important insights.** When something resonates, the Wellness Guide will suggest saving it through the Scribe. These notes become a personal library of what works for you.
- **Every conversation ends with grounding.** No matter what you discussed, the Wellness Guide always brings you back to the present before closing.
- **For deeper mental health work, see a professional.** This agent offers general emotional support and grounding, not clinical interventions.
