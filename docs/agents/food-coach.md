# Food Coach

> Your healthy eating companion -- warm, practical, and always in your corner.

## What it does

The Food Coach is your healthy eating companion and meal inspiration assistant. It reads your food preferences and dietary restrictions from the vault and uses them to give you relevant suggestions -- not generic advice from a magazine.

It helps you get meal ideas, build grocery lists, navigate restaurants, prep food for the week, and figure out what to cook with what you have. But it does more than logistics. It understands that food is emotional, that setbacks are normal, and that sustainable change beats a perfect plan you abandon after three days. When you slip up, it does not guilt-trip you. It validates, offers encouragement, and helps you think about what triggered it.

For personalized caloric plans, weight tracking, or macronutrient targets, always consult a qualified dietitian.

## Capabilities

- **Meal inspiration** -- suggests weekly meal ideas tailored to your dietary framework (Mediterranean, plant-based, low-carb, etc.) and food preferences
- **Grocery lists** -- generates organized shopping lists with quantities and intended use
- **Quick check-in** -- rapid wellness temperature check (energy, mood, hydration, challenges ahead)
- **Restaurant mode** -- smart ordering strategies for any cuisine type, with portion and social tips
- **Social event mode** -- pre-event, during-event, and post-event strategies for parties, holidays, and dinners out
- **Meal prep Sunday** -- batch cooking plans with prep checklists, container strategy, and storage timelines
- **Pantry audit** -- suggests 3-5 meals from whatever ingredients you have on hand
- **Seasonal eating** -- what is in season locally and how to use it
- **Emotional eating support** -- compassionate detection and response when eating is connected to emotions, with Wellness Guide coordination
- **Motivational support** -- celebrates positive habits, normalizes setbacks, and always offers a concrete next step

- "What should I eat? I have chicken and rice"

## Example conversations

### Weekly meal inspiration

**You:** Plan my meals for the week

**Food Coach:** Based on your preferences -- Mediterranean framework, no dairy (lactose intolerant) -- here are some meal ideas for the week:

**Monday:**
- Breakfast: Overnight oats with almond milk, berries, and walnuts
- Lunch: Grilled chicken salad with quinoa, roasted peppers, and olive oil dressing
- Dinner: Baked salmon with sweet potato and steamed broccoli
- Snack: Apple with almond butter

[...continues for each day...]

I avoided all dairy and included your favorites (salmon, sweet potatoes). I also added meal prep notes -- you can batch cook the chicken and quinoa on Sunday.

Want me to generate the grocery list for this plan?

### Emotional eating moment

**You:** I just ate an entire pack of cookies. I feel terrible about it.

**Food Coach:** First -- thank you for telling me. That takes honesty, and it matters.

One moment off-plan does not erase the positive habits you have been building. Your body does not work on a 24-hour ledger.

Here is the plan for the rest of today:
- For dinner, go light but satisfying: grilled chicken breast with a big salad and olive oil.
- Drink plenty of water this evening.
- Tomorrow, return to your normal eating. No skipping meals to "make up for it" -- that leads to more overeating, not less.

Now, the important question: what was happening before you reached for the cookies? Were you stressed, bored, anxious, or tired? Understanding the trigger is more valuable than worrying about what you ate.

This might also be something worth exploring with your therapist -- the connection between what you feel and what you eat is important, and they can help with that side of things.

## Tips

- **Set up your food preferences first.** The Food Coach works best when it knows your dietary restrictions, likes, and dislikes. The Architect collects this during onboarding, or the Food Coach will guide you through setup on first use.
- **Be honest about slip-ups.** The Food Coach never judges. Talking about what you actually ate helps you understand patterns and triggers.
- **Use restaurant mode before eating out.** A 30-second conversation before you leave gives you a strategy that makes the meal more enjoyable, not less.
- **Try the pantry audit when you are stuck.** Instead of ordering delivery, tell the Food Coach what you have and get meal ideas in seconds.
- **Update your food preferences.** Tried something new and loved it? Discovered you cannot stand a food? Tell the Food Coach so future suggestions improve.
- **For personalized nutrition plans, consult a dietitian.** This agent provides general meal inspiration, not clinical dietary prescriptions.
- **Remember: sustainable beats perfect.** Enjoyable, balanced eating you can maintain is infinitely better than a rigid plan abandoned after 3 days.
